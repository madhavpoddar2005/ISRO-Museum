Advanced Space Technologies

PSLV (Polar Satellite Launch Vehicle)

ISRO's workhorse rocket with over 50 successful missions. Known for its reliability and cost-effectiveness in launching satellites to polar orbits.

GSLV Mark III

India's heaviest rocket capable of carrying 4-ton class satellites to geostationary transfer orbit, enabling manned space missions.

Reusable Launch Vehicle

Developing technology for cost-effective space access through reusable spacecraft, reducing launch costs significantly.

NavIC (IRNSS)

India's own regional satellite navigation system, providing accurate position information over India and surrounding regions, similar to GPS.

Cryogenic Engine Technology

Advanced propulsion technology using super-cooled fuels, enabling heavier payloads to be launched into higher orbits.

Space Capsule Recovery Experiment (SRE)

Demonstrated India's ability to recover an orbiting space capsule safely, paving the way for future human spaceflight missions.

High Throughput Satellites (HTS)

Satellites like GSAT-6A and GSAT-29 provide high-speed internet and communication services across India, including remote areas.

Semi-Cryogenic Engine

Next-generation propulsion system using refined kerosene and liquid oxygen, designed to increase payload capacity and reduce launch costs for future rockets.

Satellite Laser Communication

Cutting-edge technology for high-speed, secure data transfer between satellites and ground stations using laser beams, enabling faster and more reliable communications.
