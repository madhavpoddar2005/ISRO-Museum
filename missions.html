Major Space Missions (2014-2024)

Aryabhata

1975

India's first satellite, launched by the Soviet Union. Aryabhata marked India's entry into the space age and was used for scientific and technological research.

Type: Experimental
Launch Vehicle: Kosmos-3M
Achievement: First Indian satellite

Bhaskara-I

1979

Bhaskara-I was India's first experimental remote sensing satellite, launched to study earth resources, land, and water using TV and microwave sensors.

Type: Remote Sensing
Launch Vehicle: Intercosmos
Achievement: First Indian earth observation satellite

SLV-3 (Rohini RS-1)

1980

India's first indigenously developed launch vehicle, SLV-3, successfully placed the Rohini RS-1 satellite in orbit, making India the sixth country to launch a satellite on its own rocket.

Type: Launch Vehicle
Payload: Rohini RS-1
Achievement: First Indian launch vehicle

GSAT-1

2001

GSAT-1 was the first satellite in the GSAT series, launched to augment the INSAT system for telecommunication and broadcasting services in India.

Type: Communication
Launch Vehicle: GSLV-D1
Achievement: First GSAT satellite

Cartosat-2

2007

Cartosat-2 is a high-resolution earth observation satellite used for cartographic applications, urban and rural planning, and disaster management support.

Type: Earth Observation
Resolution: 1 meter
Achievement: High-resolution mapping

Chandrayaan-1

2008

Chandrayaan-1 was India's first mission to the Moon. It made a major discovery of water molecules on the lunar surface and marked India's entry into deep space exploration.

Type: Lunar Orbiter
Duration: 312 days
Achievement: Discovered water on the Moon

RISAT-1

2012

RISAT-1 was India's first indigenous radar imaging satellite, providing all-weather surveillance for agriculture, forestry, and disaster management.

Type: Earth Observation
Payload: Synthetic Aperture Radar
Achievement: First Indian radar imaging satellite

Mars Orbiter Mission (Mangalyaan)

2014

India's first interplanetary mission successfully entered Mars orbit, making ISRO the fourth space agency to reach Mars. The mission cost a mere $74 million, making it the most cost-effective Mars mission to date.

Duration: Still Operational
Cost: $74 Million
Achievement: First Asian nation to reach Mars orbit

Chandrayaan-2

2019

A complex mission consisting of an orbiter, lander, and rover to explore the lunar south pole. While the lander experienced a hard landing, the orbiter continues to provide valuable scientific data from lunar orbit.

Orbiter: Operational
Objectives: Lunar surface studies
Significance: First attempt to land near south pole

Chandrayaan-3

2023

India's successful lunar landing mission that made history by becoming the first country to land near the Moon's south pole. The Pragyan rover conducted surface experiments and confirmed the presence of sulfur.

Landing: Successful
Duration: 14 Earth days
Achievement: First south pole landing

Aditya-L1

2023

India's first solar mission to study the Sun's corona, solar emissions, solar winds, and flares. Positioned at the L1 Lagrange point, it provides continuous observation of solar activities.

Position: L1 Lagrange Point
Objectives: Solar corona studies
Significance: First Indian solar observatory

Gaganyaan G1

2025 (Planned)

Gaganyaan G1 is the first uncrewed test flight of India's human spaceflight program. It will demonstrate the crew module, life support, and mission systems in preparation for future crewed missions.

Type: Uncrewed Test Flight
Launch Vehicle: GSLV Mk III
Achievement: First step towards human spaceflight

Mangalyaan 2

Planned

Mangalyaan 2 (Mars Orbiter Mission 2) is ISRO's planned second mission to Mars, aiming to build on the success of Mangalyaan with advanced scientific instruments and extended mission objectives.

Type: Mars Orbiter
Launch Vehicle: GSLV Mk III (Planned)
Achievement: Next step in Mars exploration
